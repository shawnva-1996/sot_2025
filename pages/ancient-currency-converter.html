💸 Abraham’s Purchase of Machpelah

📖 In Genesis 23:16, Abraham secured a burial site for Sarah by paying 400 shekels of silver to Ephron the Hittite. This wasn’t just a deal—it was the first recorded land purchase in the promised land, cementing a spiritual foothold for generations.

⚖️ A biblical shekel weighed approximately 11.4 grams. At today’s silver price ($0.50–$1.30 per gram):

  • 💰 1 shekel ≈ $6 to $15 USD
  • 💰 400 shekels ≈ $2,400 to $6,000 USD in modern silver value

📍 The field and cave of Machpelah lie near Hebron, located in the southern West Bank—approximately 30 km south of Jerusalem. Today, the site is known as the Cave of the Patriarchs 🕍 by Jews and the Ibrahimi Mosque 🕌 by Muslims.

🏛️ While the inner cave remains sealed, the monumental structure above dates to Herod’s reign, serving over centuries as a synagogue, church, and mosque. It remains one of the holiest shared sacred spaces on Earth.

🧬 Within this tomb rest the founding figures of faith: Abraham and Sarah, Isaac and Rebekah, and Jacob and Leah—a legacy literally grounded in silver and belief.
